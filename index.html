What I will fight for
in District 11

01

Transparency & Anti-Corruption

Regular unannounced inspections of publicly funded programs. Full public records as a right, not a privilege. Every public dollar publicly accounted for — because accountability is not optional, it is a duty.

Accountability First
02

Real Constituent Engagement

Town halls across LD11 — year-round, not just at election time. I come to you, listen, document, act, and report back. Representation means presence, not just a distant vote cast on your behalf.

Serve the People
03

Debt Relief & Economic Justice

Caldwell families carry crushing medical and consumer debt. I support debt jubilee frameworks and working-class relief mechanisms that give ordinary District 11 residents a genuine fresh start.

Families First
04

Reindustrialization & Local Jobs

Canyon County's economy should be built by Canyon County workers. I support bringing skilled trades and real manufacturing back — so prosperity is grown here, not imported from outside.

Idaho First
05

Constitutional Fidelity

Idaho's Constitution is the supreme law of this state. I will work to codify and centralize state statute so every citizen can understand the laws that govern them — and hold government strictly accountable to them.

Rule of Law
06

Family & Community First

Parents are the primary advocates for their children. I support parental rights, investment in local parks, recreation, and health — the community infrastructure that makes Caldwell worth raising a family in.

What I Believe

Seek truth from facts.
Then act on what you find.

I believe every system — no matter how entrenched — can be shaken and renewed. The old gives way to the new. That is not idealism. That is history.

I believe the purpose of government is not to accumulate power or protect its own administration — it is to serve the people it represents, investigate what is broken, and act on what it finds. A government that has stopped asking hard questions has stopped working.

I believe in the dignity of working people. In the right of every family to know the laws that govern them. In the obligation of every elected official to show up — not just on election day, but every day after.

Canyon County is not a stepping stone. It is home. And home deserves someone who treats it that way.
